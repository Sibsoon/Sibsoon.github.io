Motivational Image: Nimbus Photography by Berndnaut Smilde

Source

The inspiration for our final project is this photography of an indoor nimbus cloud. The photography is part of a whole series of indoor cloud photographies, made by the dutch artist Berndnaut Smilde. Our goal is to render an indoor cloud in a setting inspired by Berndnaut Smilde's photographies. Also, we would like to add another interesting element to the scene: a lightning coming out of the cloud.
Rendering a lightning was our initial idea for this year's theme "Perfectly Timed". Thus, we have a secondary motivational image of a lightning, shown below. It suits the theme very well, since a lightning is only visible for a short moment. Moreover, we always intended to render a scene that does not exist in real life. For this reason, we decided render the cloud and the lightning in an indoor setting. Ironically, our motivational image shows that such a surreal scene can indeed exists in real life.

Relevant Features

To render such a scene, we need some key features in our renderer. The indoor setting will require different materials, light setup, and textures with normal mapping, which we want to realize with different BSDFs, additional emitters, as well as nori support for textures with normal maps.
To adjust the scene for our specific need, we decided to model some of the key features ourselves with blender.
To get fast and good results we want to port nori to the Euler cluster. This will support the workflow as well the quality of the final image.
To render a realistic cloud, we want to implement heterogeneous volumetric participating media with progressive photon mapping. For some more realistic detail, we additionally want to realize spectral rendering.
As for post processing of the image, we want to use some moderate denoising, to not only rely on pure computational power for a high quality result.
